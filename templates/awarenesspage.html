Breast Cancer Awareness

What is Breast Cancer?

Breast cancer is a disease where cells in the breast grow uncontrollably. It primarily affects women, though men can develop it too. It is one of the most common cancers globally.

Why Awareness Matters

  • Helps in recognizing early signs and symptoms.
  • Encourages regular medical checkups and screenings.
  • Promotes open conversations and education about breast health.
  • Supports research, funding, and community outreach.

Early Warning Signs

  • New lump in the breast or underarm.
  • Swelling, irritation, or dimpling of the skin.
  • Change in breast shape or size.
  • Redness or flakiness around the nipple.
  • Unusual nipple discharge (other than breast milk).
  • Persistent pain in any part of the breast.

Breast Cancer Awareness Month

October is globally observed as Breast Cancer Awareness Month. Campaigns during this time aim to spread awareness, share survivor stories, raise funds for research, and encourage early screening. The pink ribbon is the universal symbol of support.

Key Message

“Early detection saves lives. Be aware. Stay informed.”
